Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khah

1936 - 2021

Father of Pakistan’s Nuclear Program

Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan is remembered as the man who turned Pakistan’s dream of security and dignity into reality. Born in 1936, he rose from a humble beginning to become the architect of Pakistan’s nuclear strength. With quiet confidence and unshakable patriotism, he built the uranium enrichment facility at Kahuta when the world doubted Pakistan’s ability. He did not work for fame or reward, but for the safety of his homeland and the generations yet to come. At a time when Pakistan stood vulnerable, his courage gave the nation a shield no enemy could break. He believed that true independence is not given by others—it is earned through knowledge, discipline and sacrifice. For this reason, the people call him “Mohsin-e-Pakistan,” the benefactor who strengthened the nation’s backbone. His life is a lesson that one determined individual, guided by purpose, can change the destiny of millions. Though he passed away in 2021, his legacy lives in every heart that beats for Pakistan. Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan will forever be honoured as the Father of Pakistan’s Nuclear Program.

Biography

  • Full Name: Dr. Abdul Qadeer Khan
  • Born: 1 April 1936, Bhopal, British India
  • Died: 10 October 2021, Islamabad, Pakistan
  • Education: Studied Metallurgical Engineering in Europe – University of Berlin, Delft University of Technology (Netherlands), and Catholic University of Leuven (Belgium).
  • Professional Work: Worked at URENCO in the Netherlands, later founded Khan Research Laboratories (KRL) in Pakistan.
  • Major Achievement: Led Pakistan’s uranium enrichment program and made Pakistan a nuclear power.
  • Title of Honour: Known as “Mohsin-e-Pakistan” and “Father of Pakistan’s Nuclear Program”.
  • Awards: Recipient of Nishan-e-Imtiaz (twice) and Hilal-e-Imtiaz.
  • Legacy: A symbol of national pride, scientific excellence, and self-reliance for Pakistan.
